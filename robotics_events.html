RULES & REGULATIONS

GENERAL PILOT RULES


• All participants must be a registered member and adhere to all rules and regulations of safe airframe operation of flight.
• NO FPV’S ARE ALLOWED. FRAME SIZE SHOULD LIE BETWEEN 350 TO 500MM.
• All pilots must have Fail-Safe mode on their drones.
• All pilots must have an “ARMING” position switch or sequence on their radio.
• All drones should control manually.
• GPS stabilization should not be present.

JUDGING AND FIELD MARSHALING :


• All races will be governed by our judging committee.
• Each race will be monitored by judges and field marshals to maintain fair and accurate competition.
• Any practice or behaviour that is deemed unsafe will result in an immediate disqualification.

FIELD / COURSE OPERATIONS :


• All flights should be grounded if there are any ongoing activity on the field.
• Flight paths should have a safety buffer zone with a minimum of 20 meters from any spectator or building.

COURSE RULES OF ENGAGEMENT


• Pilots must keep all aircraft in the disarmed state until they have been given the “ARM” signal. (This will happen only when the aircraft has been placed on the starting deck and all field staff have left the area.)
• Pilots must adhere to the prescribed launch position. No movement before the starting signal. False starts will incur a penalty.
• Pilots must maintain control of their aircraft at all times and only fly within their skill level. Any pilot who exhibits unsafe flying procedures may be disqualified.
• Once pilots have successfully completed the race course, they must return to the start/finish pad, land and DISARM.
• Pilots that have crashed at any point during their race, will be given a second chance to resume race from the start point but with some fixed penalty points.
• Pilots must successfully fly through all gates, flags, and other obstacles on the course. If an obstacle is missed, the pilot must safely turn around and attempt the obstacle again.
• If a pilot lose control on their aircraft, the pilot must attempt a safe landing, cut throttle in a safe area or execute a failsafe procedure in a safe area

(NOTE: College or the Organisers are not responsible for any type of damage of your Drone and spare parts will not be provided here.)

There will be only one round:
Round 1: Final Round
